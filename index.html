Our Story

More than a hotel. A piece of Piassa.

The Foundation

Founded in 1954 E.C. by Eyasu Asfaw and his wife Asefwash Kinfu, tsgereda Hotel began as a vision to create a welcoming sanctuary in the heart of Piassa â€” blending mid-century Ethiopian-Italian fusion architecture with the warmth of Habesha hospitality.

The name "tsgereda" or "Red Rose" came from the abundance of red flowers that once grew around the property, symbolizing love, heritage, and the blooming spirit of Addis Ababa.

The Modern Era

After the passing of Eyasu Asfaw and Asefwash Kinfu, management was transferred to Tadesse Eyasu, who continues preserving the family's legacy and the history of this beloved establishment.

Today, tsgereda Hotel stands as a testament to generational leadership â€” preserving its heritage while embracing the future with new ventures like the upcoming VIP Sky Lounge.

Historical Timeline

A journey through time.

Before 1935

The Residence

The building served as the residence of a small Ethiopian government official — a quiet home in the growing neighborhood of Piassa.

1935 – 1941

Italian Occupation

During the Italian occupation, the property was occupied by Italian authorities and used as a library — a quiet witness to a turbulent period in Ethiopian history.

1941

Liberation

After Ethiopia's victory, the property returned to Ethiopian ownership — ready for a new chapter.

1954 E.C.

tsgereda is Born

Acquired by Eyasu Asfaw and Asefwash Kinfu, it became one of the best-known bars and restaurants in Piassa. The red roses that bloomed around the property gave it its name — tsgereda, the Red Rose.

Post 1954 E.C. — 2016 E.C.

Tadesse Eyasu — Steward of the Legacy

After the founders, Tadesse Eyasu carried forward the family legacy, preserving the history and traditions that make tsgereda unique for future generations.

2016 E.C. — Present

New Generation Leadership

Under the administration of Yonatan Eyasu since 2016 E.C., tsgereda Hotel continues to honor its heritage while embracing a new era of hospitality and innovation.
